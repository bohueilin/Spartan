What To Share For A WHOOP-Like Health Telemetry UX

Pick what you can provide now. We can still design with partial inputs.

Most Useful Inputs

1

Reference Screenshots

WHOOP, Apple Health, Google Fit/Fitbit, Oura, Garmin, or any telemetry app screens you like. Home, detail, journal, report, and alert screens are especially useful.

2

Your Metric Inventory

Which signals matter most: glucose, TG/HDL, ApoB, Lp(a), CAC, BP, RHR, HRV, sleep, stress, symptoms, workouts, nutrition, labs, imaging, notes.

3

Your Daily Ritual

When you open the app, what question should it answer first: readiness, risk radar, metabolic focus, stress load, training plan, or weekly momentum?

4

Tone And Safety Boundaries

Examples of language you like and dislike. Especially useful because this app must feel supportive without diagnosing or prescribing.

5

Visual Taste

Dark vs light, dense vs calm, rings vs cards, color-coded status vs neutral clinical style, graph-heavy vs explanation-heavy.

6

Privacy And Export Expectations

What you want to export for a clinician, what should stay hidden, and how much confirmation you want before sharing/deleting health data.

Design North Star

Vital Compass should become a local-first telemetry cockpit

WHOOP-like: fast status, personal baselines, trend deviation, behavior journal, recovery/stress/metabolic context.

Apple-like: privacy, calm hierarchy, clarity, restraint, no dark patterns around health data.

Google/Material-like: native Android ergonomics, accessible components, adaptive layouts, clear motion and state.
